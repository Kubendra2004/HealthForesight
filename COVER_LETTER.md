In the current healthcare landscape, efficient resource allocation and accurate clinical decision-making are paramount. However, many healthcare facilities, particularly in resource-constrained settings, continue to rely on reactive, manual processes and fragmented legacy systems. These limitations often result in bed shortages, increased patient wait times, and a lack of data-driven insights for proactive care planning.

Our research addresses these critical challenges by developing **HealthForesight**, a comprehensive, open-source healthcare management system. Unlike traditional platforms that operate in silos, our system uniquely integrates:
1.  **Explainable Machine Learning**: Implementing predictive models for heart disease, diabetes, and readmission risk with SHAP-based interpretability to foster clinical trust.
2.  **Proactive Resource Forecasting**: Utilizing Prophet-based time-series modeling to provide accurate 7-day forecasts for bed occupancy, ICU capacity, and essential supplies.
3.  **Unified Operations**: Streamlining workflows through role-specific dashboards for patients, doctors, and administrators, built on a robust hybrid database architecture.

Our work demonstrates significant operational improvements, including a **40% reduction in patient check-in times** and a **75% improvement in forecasting accuracy** over traditional methods. Furthermore, we present a cost-effective implementation strategy that bridges the gap between advanced technology and accessibility for smaller healthcare providers.
